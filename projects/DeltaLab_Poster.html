The 2024 Presidential Debate's Effects on Campaign Spending

Tool — Python · R
Year — 2024

Overview

As a part of the DeltaLab, in collaboration with the Wesleyan Media Project, I worked with large amounts of data concerning presidential election campaigns. For this project, my team and I were especially interested in the first presidential debate and its effects on each candidate's presidential campaigns.

What I found

There were quite a few differences in how the Harris and Trump campaigns chose to direct their spending. Harris outspent Trump by a very large margin. Interestingly though, following the debate, Trump began to outspend Harris. The candidates also had different campaign strategies. Both had a focus on the general campaign and the economy. Harris specifically focused heavily on abortion, healthcare and foreign policy. Interestingly, both candidates' spending on democracy increased greatly after the election, with Harris' spending almost doubling and Trump's more than quadrupling.

Process

We chose to go about the process of classifying ad topics in two different ways. The first was through the GPT 4o-mini LLM, and the other was through a hard-coded dictionary approach in which certain words were categorized pertaining to distinct categories. We then split up the data from before and after the debate and mapped out the spending quantity and categorizations.
